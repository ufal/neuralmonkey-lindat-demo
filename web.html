Models for End-to-End NLP Tasks

In many natural language processing tasks, deep learning allowed replacing pipelines of rule-based and machine learning step by monolitical models trained end-to-end. The input of the model are directly words or characters wihtout any further pre-processing.

This demo shows several tasks where deep neural network recently became a dominant approach. In particular thery are machine translation, sentiment analysis, image captioning and text summarization.

The model were trained using Neural Monkey, an open-source toolkit for sequential learning that is developed at Institute of Formal and Applied Linguistic at Charles University. On top of this demo, the models are available for download. They should serve both educational purposes and be used for studying the language representation that emerges in deep neural networks.
